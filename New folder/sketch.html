SKETCHING

By: Sketcher Aishay

Being a sketch artist is not just about drawing; itâ€™s about observing the world
around you and translating that observation into art. Whether you sketch for
personal enjoyment, as a professional artist, or as part of a creative process in
fields like concept art or illustration, mastering sketching takes time, practice,
and a deep understanding of technique and composition.

A sketching course teaches the foundational techniques for drawing and illustration.
Students learn about different types of pencils, charcoal, and other sketching materials.
The course covers basic shapes and forms, helping students build the ability to draw
from observation. Proportions, perspective, and depth are emphasized to create realistic
sketches. Light and shadow techniques are introduced to add dimension and contrast
to drawings. Students practice sketching both still life and live models to hone their
skills. The course often includes studies in gesture drawing, enhancing the fluidity of
sketches. Different styles, such as realism or abstract sketching, are explored Feedback
from instructors helps refine students' drawing techniques. By the end of the course,
students gain confidence in sketching and can create detailed, expressive drawings.
